A short story of how what2Eat came to be...

The idea of What2Eat started on a university campus, because of boredom and the sheer goal to make extra money as a broke student.
Our story begins 2 years ago... Strap in.

Skip to team
March 2019
I introduced a game to my flatmates. Rules were simple, throw a dart onto a world map and I'd cook food from that country.
I ended up falling in love with cooking and decided to perfect my skills so that I could potentially sell to students on campus.

Keep watching. I got better!

January 2021
I recruited Aditya (co-founder) and was introduced to Abdulrazaq (current Chief Technical Officer). We disgusted the possibility of creating an app that would streamline customer orders.

Around this time I realised that I wanted to help others who were good at cooking or baking and wanted to make extra money at university.

“We were essentially the Instagram police and called out fake influencers left, right and centre. It was fun and all, but there is only so many bikini chicks you can look at on Instagram before you start losing your mind.”

Dan, Co-founder and CEO of Lumio

March 2021
One weekend, I returned home to Jollof rice from a woman's home kitchen. She had packaged in a plastic bag.

“Mama where did you get this rice in huge quantities from and why is it in a plastic bag?.”

Hakim, CEO of What2Eat

I found out that people beyond the university campus were selling food from their homes trying to make ends meet regardless of what equipment they had at their disposal.

April 2021
This lead me on a unexpected journey exploring different cuisines with my friends and talking to potential cooks.

Whilst exploring the possibility of there being more home cooks we discovered that most of the people we encountered were cooking cuisines from their home countries i.e. Portuguese food, Ugandan, Jamaican etc...

This made me realise that we could help more than just the home cooks, but also authentic small food stalls and restaurants that weren't getting recognition for their ethnic food.

November 2021
We got so much great feedback about our concept and buzz that we decided to pre-launch the company at Europes biggest business show event.
Read our article by manuela willbold
Today
Today What2Eat is still discovering new cooks and showcasing their talents to the UK. Focusing on bringing culture and food together as part of our mission to put the world on your plate.

You've heard our story, it's now time for the world to hear yours.
